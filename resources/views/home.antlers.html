Bringing next-gen bundling to Statamic

On June 30th, Laravel took a bold step forward by officially stepping away from using Webpack as it's default Javascript bundler / asset compiler, in favor of Vite. This is a game changer that should make any progressive-minded developer happy, though it may cause some frustrations in a lot of you who use Laravel-based applications that haven't offered a simple means of upgrading your projects.

Statamic is one of my favorite CMS applications, and so far I haven't seen a package offering the necessary configuration to utilize Vite in a Statamic project with the most current official integration of Laravel and Vite. It took me a few hours, and my girlfriend and her cat put up with some intense bouts of spontaneous cursing, but I got this together, and learned a good deal about Vite and the newest trends in progressive web application development with ES modules in the process.

Enjoy!
